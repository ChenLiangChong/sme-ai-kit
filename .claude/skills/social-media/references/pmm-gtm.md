# PMM Gate 6: GTM Planning

Channel strategy, campaign planning, sales enablement, and go-to-market execution.

---

## GTM Planning Workflow

1. Define launch goals (pipeline $, MQLs, signups, awareness)
2. Identify target segments (from market analysis + ICP)
3. Map buyer journey (awareness → consideration → decision → retention)
4. Select channels (owned, earned, paid — prioritize by segment)
5. Create campaign briefs (messaging, assets, timeline, budget)
6. Build measurement framework (KPIs per channel, attribution)
7. Define launch tiers (Tier 1/2/3) and resource allocation
8. **Validation:** Channel mix reaches 80%+ of target segment

## Sales Enablement

Equip sales team with PMM assets:

1. Create sales deck (15-20 slides, visual-first)
2. Build one-pagers (product, competitive, case study)
3. Develop demo script (30-45 min with discovery)
4. Write email templates (outreach, follow-up, closing)
5. Create ROI calculator (input costs, output savings)
6. Conduct monthly enablement calls
7. Deliver quarterly training (positioning, competitive)
8. **Validation:** Sales uses assets in 80%+ of opportunities

### Sales Deck Structure

| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1-2 | Title, agenda |
| 3-4 | Company intro, problem statement |
| 5-7 | Solution, key benefits, demo |
| 8-10 | Differentiation, case study, pricing |
| 11-12 | Implementation, support, next steps |

### Demo Flow

```
1. Intro (2 min): Who we are, agenda
2. Discovery (5 min): Their needs, pain points
3. Demo (20 min): Product focused on their use case
4. Q&A (10 min): Objection handling
5. Next steps (3 min): Trial, POC, proposal
```

### Sales-Marketing Handoff

| Handoff | Frequency | Content |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Weekly sync | 30 min | Win/loss, competitive, new assets |
| Monthly enablement | 60 min | Product updates, training |
| Quarterly review | Half-day | Results, strategy, planning |

## GTM Metrics Framework

| Stage | Metric | Target | Tool |
|-------|--------|--------|------|
| Awareness | Impressions, Reach, Brand mentions | [Target] | Analytics, Social |
| Interest | Click-through rate, Website visits | [Target] | Analytics |
| Consideration | Demo requests, Content downloads, Email signups | [Target] | CRM, MA |
| Decision | Trials started, MQLs, SQLs | [Target] | CRM |
| Purchase | Conversion rate, Revenue, Deal size | [Target] | Payment, CRM |
| Retention | Churn rate, NPS, Expansion revenue | [Target] | Product, CS |

### Attribution Model

| Model | Best For | Limitation |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Last Touch | Simple attribution | Ignores awareness channels |
| First Touch | Understanding discovery | Ignores conversion channels |
| Linear | Equal credit across touches | Oversimplifies |
| Time Decay | Weights recent touches more | Still somewhat arbitrary |
| Data-Driven | Most accurate | Requires volume of data |

## International Expansion

Enter new markets systematically:

1. Validate market demand (inbound leads, TAM analysis)
2. Localize website, pricing, legal
3. Establish sales coverage (hire or agency)
4. Adapt messaging for cultural fit
5. Build local partnerships and references
6. Launch localized campaigns
7. Monitor CAC and conversion by market
8. **Validation:** 3+ paying customers from market in first 90 days

### Market Priority

| Market | Timeline | Budget % | Target ARR |
|--------|----------|----------|------------|
| Primary | Months 1-6 | 50% | [Target] |
| Secondary | Months 4-9 | 20% | [Target] |
| Tertiary | Months 7-12 | 15% | [Target] |

### Localization Checklist

- [ ] Website translation (professional, not machine)
- [ ] Currency and pricing localized
- [ ] Local phone number and address
- [ ] Legal compliance (GDPR, PIPEDA, etc.)
- [ ] Local payment methods
- [ ] Sales coverage during local hours
- [ ] Local case studies and references
- [ ] Cultural messaging adaptation
- [ ] Local channel mix (platforms differ by market)
