# PMM Gate 3: Messaging Creation

Value propositions, messaging frameworks, proof points, and persona-specific messaging.

---

## Messaging Framework Development

### Step-by-Step Process

1. Identify top 3 customer pain points (from market research)
2. Map product capabilities to each pain point
3. Quantify impact (time saved, cost reduced, risk avoided)
4. Craft benefit statements (outcome-focused, not feature-focused)
5. Add proof points (data, case studies, testimonials)
6. Test with 5+ customers for resonance
7. **Validation:** Customers say "that's exactly my problem"

### Messaging Hierarchy

| Level | Purpose | Length | Example |
|-------|---------|--------|---------|
| Tagline | Instant recognition | 3-5 words | "Ship faster with AI" |
| Elevator Pitch | 30-second explanation | 2-3 sentences | What + For whom + Why different |
| Value Props | Why to buy | 3-4 bullets | Outcome-focused benefits |
| Feature Messages | How it works | Per feature | Capability → Benefit → Proof |
| Long-form | Deep explanation | Paragraphs | Website, sales deck, whitepapers |

## Persona-Specific Messaging

| Persona | Cares About | Don't Say | Do Say |
|---------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Economic Buyer | ROI, risk, competitive advantage | Technical details, implementation | Business outcomes, competitive edge, risk reduction |
| Technical Buyer | Architecture, security, integration | Marketing fluff, vague claims | Technical specs, docs, security certifications |
| User/Champion | Ease of use, daily workflow | Enterprise features, pricing | Quick wins, time savings, frustration removal |

### Buyer Personas

| Persona | Title | Goals | Messaging |
|---------|-------|-------|-----------|
| Economic Buyer | VP, Director, Head of [Department] | ROI, team productivity, cost reduction | Business outcomes, ROI, case studies |
| Technical Buyer | Engineer, Architect, Tech Lead | Technical fit, easy integration | Architecture, security, documentation |
| User/Champion | Manager, Team Lead, Power User | Makes job easier, quick wins | UX, ease of use, time savings |

## Value Proposition Formula

Template: `[Product] helps [Target Customer] [Achieve Goal] by [Unique Approach]`

### Value Prop Validation

| Test | Method | Pass Criteria |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Clarity | Read to 5 people, ask what it means | 4/5 understand without explanation |
| Relevance | Ask target persona "does this matter to you?" | 4/5 say yes |
| Uniqueness | Ask "could this describe a competitor?" | 3/5 say no |
| Believability | Ask "do you believe this claim?" | 4/5 say yes |

## Proof Points Framework

| Type | Strength | Example | Best For |
|------|----------|---------|----------|
| Customer Quote | High | "Reduced review time from 4 hours to 20 minutes" | Landing pages, sales deck |
| Case Study | Very High | Full story: problem → solution → results | Website, sales enablement |
| Data Point | High | "87% of users report time savings in first week" | Ads, email, landing pages |
| Third-Party Validation | Very High | Analyst report, industry award, certification | Enterprise sales, PR |
| Demo | Medium | Live product demonstration | Sales calls, webinars |
| Free Trial Results | High | "Average trial user completes X in Y minutes" | PLG conversion |

## Channel-Specific Messaging

### Landing Pages

```
H1: [Primary benefit statement]
H2: [How you deliver that benefit]
Body: [3 value props with proof points]
CTA: [Action-oriented, specific]
Social Proof: [Logos, testimonials, stats]
```

### Email Sequences

| Email | Goal | Subject Line Pattern | CTA |
|-------|------|---------------------|-----|
| 1 - Intro | Awareness | Question about their pain point | Learn more |
| 2 - Value | Interest | How [peer] solved [problem] | See demo |
| 3 - Proof | Trust | [Number] results in [timeframe] | Start trial |
| 4 - Urgency | Action | Last chance / limited offer | Sign up now |

### Ad Copy

| Format | Headline | Description | CTA |
|--------|----------|-------------|-----|
| Search | [Benefit] + [Differentiator] | [Proof point] + [Offer] | Try Free |
| Social | [Pain point question] | [Solution in 1 sentence] | Learn More |
| Display | [Bold claim] | [Social proof] | Get Started |

### Social Media

| Platform | Tone | Format | Length |
|----------|------|--------|--------|
| LinkedIn | Professional, data-driven | Insight + CTA | 150-300 words |
| Twitter/X | Conversational, punchy | Thread or single | 280 chars / thread |
| Facebook | Story-driven, relatable | Image + caption | 50-150 words |

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe as ROI: "Costs X, saves Y, net gain Z" |
| "We already have a solution" | Differentiate: "Unlike X, we [unique value]" |
| "Not the right time" | Create urgency: "Every day without = [cost of inaction]" |
| "Need to think about it" | Remove friction: "Free trial, no commitment" |
| "What about [competitor]?" | Acknowledge + differentiate: "Great product, we differ in [area]" |
| "Too complex" | Simplify: "Most users are up and running in [time]" |
| "Not sure it'll work for us" | Social proof: "[Similar company] had the same concern, here's what happened" |

## Competitive Response Scripts

| Scenario | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Competitor mentioned first | "Yes, [competitor] is good at [X]. We focus on [Y] because [customer outcome]." |
| Feature comparison request | "Here's how we compare on [criteria that matter to them]." |
| Price comparison | "Let me show you total cost of ownership including [hidden costs they miss]." |
| "They have more features" | "More features ≠ better fit. Here's what matters for your use case." |

## Proactive Triggers

- **Messaging differs across channels** → Inconsistent story confuses buyers.
- **No documented positioning** → Without clear positioning, all messaging is guesswork.
- **New persona discovered** → Create persona-specific messaging track.
- **Competitor messaging shift** → Review and differentiate your messaging.
