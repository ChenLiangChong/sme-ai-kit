# PMM Gate 5: Competitive Intelligence

Battlecards, competitive landscape analysis, win/loss tracking, and competitive monitoring.

---

## Competitive Intelligence Workflow

Build competitive knowledge base:

1. Identify tier 1 (direct), tier 2 (adjacent), tier 3 (status quo)
2. Sign up for competitor products (hands-on evaluation)
3. Monitor competitor websites, pricing, messaging
4. Analyze sales call recordings for competitor mentions
5. Read G2/Capterra reviews (pros and cons)
6. Track competitor job postings (roadmap signals)
7. Update battlecards monthly
8. **Validation:** Sales team uses battlecards in 80%+ competitive deals

### Competitive Tier Structure

| Tier | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| 1 | Direct competitor, same category | [Competitor A, B] |
| 2 | Adjacent solution, overlapping use case | [Alt Solution C, D] |
| 3 | Status quo (what they do today) | Spreadsheets, manual, in-house |

## Battlecard Template

```
COMPETITOR: [Name]
OVERVIEW: Founded [year], Funding [stage], Size [employees], HQ [location]
WEBSITE: [URL]
PRICING: [Model + price points]

POSITIONING:
- They say: "[Their claim]"
- Reality: [Your assessment based on hands-on evaluation]

TARGET CUSTOMER:
- [Their ICP description]
- Overlap with our ICP: [High/Medium/Low]

STRENGTHS:
1. [What they do well + evidence]
2. [What they do well + evidence]
3. [What they do well + evidence]

WEAKNESSES:
1. [Where they fall short + evidence]
2. [Where they fall short + evidence]
3. [Where they fall short + evidence]

OUR ADVANTAGES:
1. [Your advantage + evidence/data]
2. [Your advantage + evidence/data]
3. [Your advantage + evidence/data]

WHEN WE WIN:
- [Scenario/profile where you consistently win]
- [Key decision criteria that favor you]

WHEN WE LOSE:
- [Scenario/profile where they win]
- [Key decision criteria that favor them]

LAND MINES (questions to plant early):
1. "Ask them about [weakness area]..."
2. "Compare their [feature] with our [feature]..."

TALK TRACK:
Objection: "[Common objection when competing with them]"
Response: "[Your response with proof point]"

RECENT CHANGES:
- [Date]: [What changed — pricing, feature, positioning]

LAST UPDATED: [Date]
```

### Monthly Tracking

| Metric | This Month | Last Month | Trend |
|--------|-----------|------------|-------|
| Overall Win Rate | X% | X% | ↑/↓ |
| Win Rate vs. [Competitor A] | X% | X% | ↑/↓ |
| Win Rate vs. [Competitor B] | X% | X% | ↑/↓ |
| Win Rate vs. Status Quo | X% | X% | ↑/↓ |
| #1 Win Reason | [Reason] | [Reason] | — |
| #1 Loss Reason | [Reason] | [Reason] | — |

### Win/Loss Interview Questions

1. "What alternatives did you evaluate?"
2. "What was the #1 factor in your decision?"
3. "What almost made you choose [competitor]?"
4. "What would have changed your mind?"
5. "How did pricing compare across options?"
6. "Who was involved in the decision?"
7. "What was the status quo before evaluating?"
8. "How did you first hear about each option?"

### Analysis Framework

Track and categorize:
- **Win reasons**: Product fit, ease of use, price, support, brand, integration
- **Loss reasons**: Missing feature, price, relationship, incumbent, timing
- **Action items**: For product team, sales team, marketing team

## Competitive Response Playbook

### When Competitor Launches New Feature

1. **Assess impact** (same day): Does this affect our differentiation?
2. **Internal brief** (24 hours): Update sales team on what it means
3. **Talking points** (48 hours): Prepare response if customers ask
4. **Battlecard update** (1 week): Incorporate into competitive materials
5. **Strategic response** (2 weeks): Decide if product/positioning change needed

### When Competitor Changes Pricing

1. **Document changes**: Old price vs. new price, packaging changes
2. **Analyze impact**: Does this affect our price positioning?
3. **Update comparisons**: Revise any pricing comparison materials
4. **Assess response**: Do we need to adjust? (Usually no — avoid reactive pricing)
5. **Brief sales**: Provide talking points for price objections

### When Competitor Gets Press Coverage

1. Don't panic — media coverage ≠ market share
2. Analyze the angle: What narrative are they pushing?
3. Prepare counter-narrative if journalists reach out
4. Accelerate your own PR timeline if relevant
5. Use as motivation, not as a trigger for reactive changes

## Proactive Triggers

- **Competitor repositioning detected** → Review your positioning.
- **New competitor enters market** → Add to competitive landscape, assess tier.
- **Win rate dropping vs. specific competitor** → Deep dive, update battlecard.
- **Sales team asking "how do we compete with X?"** → Battlecard needed.
